About the ACM

The Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) is an international learned society for computing. It was founded in 1947, and is the world's largest scientific and educational computing society.The ACM is a non-profit professional membership group, claiming nearly 100,000 student and professional members as of 2019. Its headquarters are in New York City. The ACM is an umbrella organization for academic and scholarly interests in computer science. Its motto is "Advancing Computing as a Science & Profession".

Project Classes

Purpose

Southeastern's computer science is one of the few programs that offer many real world ready courses. These courses are designed to get students familiar with the AGILE design process. Students are allowed to choose their own client and projects (excluding CMPS 383), and even their own desired technologies to use. Ultimately, these courses are also designed to enhance the students ability to learn new technologies and adapt to working in sprints showing constant progress each iteration.

285 is a student's first project class. Groups of 3-4 are chosen and are tasked with creating a piece software. Expectations are not usually set too high for this class, however, that should not stop you from working hard!

383 is course that is overseen by Envoc a local software development company. In this course the groups are chosen at random by the professor, and the project itself is given to the students by Envoc. Student can expect to work with ASP.NET, React, and Xamarin or React Native

411 is typically a student's final project class. The course is setup the same way as 285. However, since a student is usually around the senior level at this point the standards are way up, so work hard to impress the industry.
